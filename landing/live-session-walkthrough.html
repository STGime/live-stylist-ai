What a live AI styling session actually looks like

The shortest way to explain LiveStylist is to show one. Below is a real five-minute live session, screen by screen — the moment the app opens, the first time the AI sees the user's look, the suggestions, the preview images, and the final outfit it lands on. If you've ever wondered what "live AI stylist" means concretely instead of as marketing copy, this is it.

The session, screen by screen

Same flow as the video, walked through carefully. Read it like a transcript.

0:00 — The app opens. The AI greets you by name. Because LiveStylist uses an anonymous device ID instead of a login, there's no sign-in screen between you and a session.

0:08 — Hey Nicole. A familiar, casual opener. The AI takes the time to acknowledge you before getting to the work — this is a conversation, not a form to fill.

0:15 — Pick the vibe. Tell the AI what you're aiming for. Date night? Office? Weekend? The session is calibrated to the occasion from the first second.

0:25 — Start. Tap once. The live vision pipeline spins up. Three vision agents (eye / mouth / outfit) start analysing your front-facing camera simultaneously, and the voice agent takes over the conversation.

1:20 — Let me show you a preview. When the AI suggests a change, it doesn't just describe it — it generates a preview image alongside the voice. You see what it means.

1:55 — Soft brown liner. The advice is concrete: not "maybe try makeup", but "soft brown liner across the lash line". Specific enough to act on, gentle enough not to feel prescribed.

2:20 — Enhance the eyes. A new suggestion, framed as a reason rather than a rule: the AI explains why — because the warmth of your top picks up the colour of your iris.

2:45 — Result preview. Side-by-side: before and after the suggested change, rendered as a preview image. You make the call.

3:10 — A little sparkle. Small finishing detail: an earring suggestion to balance the upper half of the outfit. The AI is calibrating the noise level, not just the volume.

3:35 — I love the suggestion. You can agree with the AI or push back. Both are normal. The session is a conversation, and the AI revises in real time when you disagree.

4:00 — Fresh look. Halfway through, a stocktake: the AI restates where you've landed so far, makes sure you still agree, and asks what else to refine.

4:45 — Compliments and advice. The closing minute is direct, warm, and substantive. Not gushing — useful. Whatever genuinely works gets named; whatever doesn't is noted with a reason.

5:00 — The final look. Session ends. You get a one-screen summary you can save to style memory. Next session picks up here — the AI remembers what worked and refines its advice across sessions.

What was happening under the hood

While the conversation felt like one continuous voice, the system was running three things at once. A live vision pipeline (multi-agent: eye, mouth, outfit) analysed the camera frames every few seconds, processed in memory and discarded — nothing was written to disk. A live voice agent (Google Gemini Live) handled the dialogue with roughly 300ms response latency, so it felt like a person you could interrupt. And a preview-image generator (Fal.ai, Gemini 2.5 Flash Image) rendered the suggested looks alongside the conversation so suggestions were visual, not just verbal.

That triple-track is what makes LiveStylist's sessions feel like a video call with a stylist rather than a chat with a bot. It's also what no other AI stylist app in 2026 is doing.

How long it actually takes

Five minutes, every time, by design. We tried longer sessions in early builds — they were worse. The five-minute box forces the AI to be useful immediately and forces you to take the advice instead of debating it forever. The pricing reflects this: Premium gives you thirty 5-minute sessions a month, which is roughly one a day. Most people use far fewer.

Common questions

How long is a live styling session?

Five minutes. Time-boxed deliberately — long enough for real advice, short enough to fit into the moment you're actually getting ready.

Can I interrupt the AI mid-sentence?

Yes. The voice agent supports natural turn-taking. Cut it off, change direction, ask a follow-up — it adapts.

Do I have to look perfect to start?

No. The whole point is to start from wherever you are. Pyjamas, bedhead, mid-getting-ready — the AI doesn't judge and nothing is stored.
